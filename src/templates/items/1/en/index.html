Spherical vessel with a short cylindrical neck and flatly everted outer rim. To form the rim, the upper part of the neck was first turned outwards and then folded back inside and rolled in (the width of the fold from the edge of the mouth is 0.5-0.6 cm). Along the rolled interior edge are burrs and gaps. The neck abruptly expands into a spherical body without a pronounced base. Around the central part of the circumference of the body are distinct traces of a three-tier frieze consisting of vertically extended rhombuses. In most cases the outlines of the rhombuses are defined by very fine horizontal scratches filled with a dark material; in some places the traces of the rhombuses are very faint, and in others the impressions outlining the rhombuses are visible. This pattern of rhomboid decoration probably had some functional purpose - for attaching some sort of braiding, evidently made of organic material, with adhesive material, remains of which are preserved in the form of a dark substance. The lower part of the body is separated by a circle consisting of nine impressions of various depths. On one side near the neck, there is an etched zigzag of five and a half lines punched with a tool with a sharp, flat working edge 0.3 cm long. The vessel is dented and has a crack in it. The neck is also dented and has been restored. There are scratches and dents on the body. There are patches of a dark substance on the surface.

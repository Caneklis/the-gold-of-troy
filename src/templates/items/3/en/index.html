A boat-shaped vessel with a horizontal rising spout at each end. The sides and spouts are asymmetrical. Both spouts have somewhat flattened openings with sharply everted projections, but one of the spouts has its sides pressed together, giving it an elliptical shape and an incompletely closed mouth. The other is wide open; it seems it had once been compressed and was then reopened. The upper edge of the vessel has a projecting interior rim with a roughly triangular cross-section. Along the axis of the exterior of the vessel is a long, hammered, raised, longitudinal roll-shaped rib, which in the center flares into an almond-shaped base. Corresponding to this in the interior of the vessel is an indented groove running along the whole length of the relief rib. The interior surface retains clearly visible marks from the tools used to shape the vessel; there are also numerous fine longitudinal scratches. The outside surface is substantially smoother, without pronounced traces of hammering.
